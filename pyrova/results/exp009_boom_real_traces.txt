    config 1: corr(FP,INT)=-0.289  corr(FP,MEM)=-0.106  corr(INT,MEM)=+0.292  CV=0.257  (same smallboom areas — power only)
    config 2: corr(FP,INT)=-0.293  corr(FP,MEM)=-0.105  corr(INT,MEM)=+0.270  CV=0.257  (same smallboom areas — power only)

    -> real workloads DO show anti-correlated functional clusters (FP/INT, FP/MEM) — the structure the theory needs (gcc was the wrong, single-program test).
    mechanism: FP cluster carries 1.5% of total power (max 16.6% in any program); hotspot cell is identical in 50/80 programs at the original layout (30 programs move it).

    -> no significant benefit (CI spans 0). Consistent with the measured mechanism above: FP carries ~1.5% of power and the hotspot moves in only 30/80 programs, so the anti-correlation has little thermal leverage.
    POWER CAVEAT: OOS CVaR at alpha=0.9 on a 40-program test set averages the top ~3 programs — far below the N_TEST>=1500 the synthetic experiments use; with 80 real programs this is the best available, so treat (2) as low-powered, not as a precise null.
